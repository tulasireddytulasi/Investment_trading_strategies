This video(https://youtu.be/pCmJ8wsAS_w?list=PLUu5dK1pEfFfWoJYbdPgTL6Yef267sbW6) introduces a **trading strategy** combining **Bollinger Bands** and the **Relative Strength Index (RSI)** to identify market reversals. Here is a breakdown of the strategy:

**1. Setup & Indicators:**
* **Bollinger Bands:** The creator recommends using default settings (length 20, standard deviation 2) to visualize support and resistance (0:44-1:18).
* **RSI:** Set the RSI to a 14-period length. The strategy focuses on identifying overbought (above 70) and oversold (below 30) conditions (1:30-1:55).

**2. Core Concept (Mean Reversion):**
* The strategy relies on **mean reversion**, the idea that price eventually returns to its moving average after reaching extremes (2:16-2:55).
* **Entry Rules:**
    * **Long Trade:** Enter when the price drops below the lower Bollinger Band AND the RSI is oversold (below 30) (3:28-3:43).
    * **Short Trade:** Enter when the price rises above the upper Bollinger Band AND the RSI is overbought (above 70) (3:37-3:43).

**3. Avoiding Traps:**
* The narrator warns against entering trades when the market is moving strongly in one direction, as indicators can stay at extremes for a long time (3:11-3:20, 4:21-4:37).
* Using the **"RSI Divergence"** technique can help identify better entry points, where the price makes a new low but the RSI makes a higher low (5:33-5:55).

According to the video, the recommended settings for the **Bollinger Bands** indicator are as follows:

* **Length:** Set the length to **20** (0:44-0:46).
* **Standard Deviation:** Set the standard deviation to **1** (0:48-0:51).

The creator also suggests removing the indicator's background color, noting that this is a personal preference (0:52-0:57).

According to the video, you can spot a bullish divergence by observing the price action in relation to the RSI indicator: the price makes a new, lower low compared to the previous low, but at the same time, the RSI makes a higher low. This indicates that the bearish momentum is weakening, signaling a potential reversal. (5:33 - 5:55)
