This video(https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JL7HdUKRxfI&t=21s) by *Trade with Pat* serves as a comprehensive, beginner-friendly guide to his **swing trading strategy**, which he has refined over 16 years of trading.

**Core Concepts:**
* **What is Swing Trading?** It is a trading style where positions are held for 24 hours to two weeks, targeting larger price moves (50–1,000+ pips) while requiring less time on the charts than day trading (1:02-1:42).
* **Technical Foundation:** Pat uses *TradingView* to analyze candlestick charts, focusing on the 1-hour, 4-hour, and daily timeframes (1:43-2:26).
* **Trend Identification:** The strategy emphasizes trading with the trend. He uses a **50-period Exponential Moving Average (EMA)** to confirm the trend direction; if price is above the EMA, he looks only for buy opportunities (2:27-4:15).

**The Strategy Rules:**
1. **Identify the Trend:** Ensure price is above the 50 EMA (2:27).
2. **Look for Pullbacks:** Wait for at least three consecutive red candles in an uptrend (4:16-4:50).
3. **Look Left:** Analyze historical support/demand levels to the left of the chart to anticipate where buyers might step in (4:51-7:31).
4. **Discounted Price:** Use the **Fibonacci Retracement** tool to identify a "discounted zone" (below the 50% level) for higher probability entries (7:32-10:09).
5. **Confirmation:** Wait for a green confirmation candle before entering the trade (12:38-13:00).

**Live Examples:**
Pat walks through multiple live trade examples (15:03-19:16), showing how he applies these rules to find entries, set stop-losses near support levels, and manage trades for profit. He concludes by reiterating that while the strategy is effective, it requires patience and discipline rather than being a "get-rich-quick" scheme (19:16-19:39).

According to the speaker, a valid trade entry is confirmed when several conditions are met within his swing trading strategy: 

* **Trend Identification:** The price must be above the 50-period Exponential Moving Average (EMA), which signifies an uptrend (03:47 - 04:15). 
* **Valid Pullback:** There should be a pullback characterized by at least three consecutive red candles (04:25 - 04:50). 
* **Discounted Pricing:** Using the Fibonacci retracement tool, the price must pull back into the "discounted zone," which is below the 50% level (08:50 - 09:30). 
* **Technical Confluence:** The trader should look to the left of the chart to identify a historical level of support or demand where buyers have previously entered the market (04:58 - 07:30). 
* **Confirmation Candle:** The final confirmation is waiting for at least one green candle to close in the direction of the trend (12:38 - 13:00).
