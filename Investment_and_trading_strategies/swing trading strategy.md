This video(https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6B8SuTq1UOw), presented by the channel *Strategy in Minutes*, provides a simplified, compressed overview and backtest of a **swing trading strategy** originally shared by *Shubham Agrawal*. The goal is to determine if the claimed potential for high returns—such as turning a small capital into a significant amount over a short period—is feasible using a disciplined approach (0:48).

### **Strategy Setup**
The strategy utilizes two primary technical analysis tools on a **one-day time frame**, specifically focusing on **mid-cap and small-cap stocks** (1:13 - 1:51):
*   **SuperTrend:** Used with default settings to identify the overall trend (Green for positive, Red for negative).
*   **23 EMA (Exponential Moving Average):** Used to identify trend momentum and as a trailing exit signal.

### **Execution Rules**
*   **Entry Criteria:** Wait for the SuperTrend to turn green and remain **flat for at least five days**. The entry occurs when the high of the swing formed during this consolidation period is broken and closed above (2:29 - 3:07).
*   **Targeting:** Traders can aim for fixed profit targets of **10% and 20%** or use the 23 EMA for a trailing stop-loss strategy (3:34 - 4:18).
*   **Stop-Loss:** The initial stop-loss is placed at the low of the swing breakout point. A trailing stop-loss is triggered when a candle closes below the 23 EMA (4:01 - 4:25).

### **Advanced Tips & Observations**
*   **Flexibility:** While designed for swing traders, the strategy can be adapted for working professionals by using alerts. If you miss the initial breakout, you can look for entries within a 2-3% dip from the breakout level (5:04 - 6:16).
*   **Limitation:** The strategy struggles during **V-shaped recoveries** because it requires a period of flat consolidation, which often causes traders to miss such sharp moves (6:18 - 6:53).
*   **Backtest Summary:** Tests on stocks like *GMR Airports* and *Jain Irrigation* demonstrate that while the strategy can produce losses, the stop-loss mechanism keeps them manageable, allowing for potential gains when a stock captures a large, sustained move (7:16 - 8:34).
