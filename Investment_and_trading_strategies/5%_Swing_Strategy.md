### **Key Concepts of the 5% Swing Strategy**
*   **The Logic:** Instead of holding indefinitely, you divide your capital into smaller chunks and invest them in index funds. When the NAV (Net Asset Value) of a specific lot rises by **5%** above your purchase price, you sell that lot to book profit and reinvest the capital into a new cycle (0:18-0:57).
*   **Portfolio Setup:** Use 15-20 index funds with zero exit loads and low TER (Total Expense Ratio). Break your total corpus (e.g., ₹10 lakhs) into smaller, staggered deployments, such as 5% of the total amount per week (1:02-1:44).
*   **Profit Booking & Discipline:** Unlike passive SIPs, this strategy turns market volatility into an opportunity. By tracking the number of days taken for a lot to hit 5%, you can calculate annualized returns and stay mentally engaged with market cycles (3:12-4:07).
*   **Managing Sideways Markets:** If your investments do not reach the 5% target, continue the staggered investment process to average down your costs. This keeps capital productive rather than letting it sit idle (4:31-5:37).

### **SIP vs. Swing Investing**
*   **SIP:** Focuses on long-term, passive compounding, but can be psychologically challenging during market downturns (7:00-7:45).
*   **Swing:** Focuses on active wealth management, liquidity, and emotional control by systematically booking and withdrawing profits (6:10-7:13).

### **Important Considerations**
*   **Taxation:** The creator emphasizes that taxes on profits are not a penalty, but evidence that the investment plan is successfully generating income (8:40-9:16).
*   **Risk Profile:** This strategy is rule-based and suitable for investors who prefer active participation. It is advised to stick to index, large-cap, or diversified funds with zero exit loads and expense ratios under 0.5% (9:47-9:57).
